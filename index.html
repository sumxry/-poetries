The Last Breath

Love killed poets,
May love also kill me.

With every word, I bleed inside,
A shattered soul, no place to hide.

My heart was open, pure, and true,
But love tore it, piece by piece, through.
They said it would heal,
But all it did was steal.

I write these lines with trembling hands,
For I know this is where it ends,
Love took my breath, my mind, my soul,
Leaving me empty, no longer whole.

Love killed poets,
And it will kill me too,
For when love dies,
There’s nothing left to do.

~summry

Her

And then… she came, so gentle, so kind,
A girl who healed the cracks in my mind.
She was a little shorter, with specs on her face,
Big, beautiful eyes filled with grace.
Her pink lips spoke words like a song,
And her cold, delicate hands held me strong.
Her short hair danced with every move,
She was the one thing I didn’t want to lose.

For the first time, I felt alive,
In her presence, I learned to survive.
She never loved me, yet I loved her still,
She became my light, my broken heart’s will.

~summry

Fading Away

I used school as a distraction,
But it didn’t ease the attraction,
Of my mind that’s stuck in place,
A heart lost in empty space.

I played songs to numb the pain,
But they only stir the rain,
The beats don’t heal, they just confuse,
Another way to feel abused.

I wrote poems from deep inside,
But now the words just run and hide,
The paper’s blank, no voice to hear,
Just silence ringing loud and clear.

I sit in the dark, hoping for light,
But it’s just me and my endless fight,
Chasing dreams that slip away,
And waiting for tomorrow, another day.

~summry

Love

Love is the fire that lights the dark,
A spark in the soul, a burning mark.
It whispers softly, it shouts aloud,
A heart beats louder, lost in the crowd.

In your eyes, I see the stars,
In your touch, I’ve traveled far.
Every kiss, a sweet refrain,
A melody that soothes my pain.

Love, it’s wild, it’s free, it’s true,
Forever lost in me and you.

~summry

Terror of Silence

The shadows crawl, the walls they speak,
Whispers from places where light won’t peek.
Eyes that follow, never seen,
Hands that touch, cold and keen.

Footsteps echo in the dead of night,
But no one’s there, just endless fright.
The air grows thick, the room feels tight,
As something stirs just out of sight.

A scream that chokes, a door that’s locked,
The terror’s real, but time’s been mocked.
In the silence, it waits, it hides,
A nightmare where no one survives.

~summry

My Girl in Green

She’s standing in the green with golden dupatta on her right
Face tilted down with eyes big and bright,

Yellow golden flowers are also in the frame
But everything fades that comes against her name,

Looking like an angel calm and sincere
Seeing her without glasses is lovely and rare.

~summry

Echoes of a Broken Soul

I am Summry, a soul lost in the dark,
A fragile boy, carrying endless scars.
Born into a house where love was a lie,
Where screams echoed, and hope would die.

I watched my mother, her tears never dry,
As my father’s rage tore her sky.
Each day he broke her with words and fists,
And I, too small, could only exist.

I prayed for it to stop, for it to end,
But prayers, it seems, aren’t meant to mend.
For years I sat in silent and death was near,
Looking for someone, to care.

Then there was Naman, my only friend,
The one who promised he’d never bend.
He saw me when no one else could,
He stayed by my side when no one would.

And then… she came, so gentle, so kind,
A girl who healed the cracks in my mind.
She was a little shorter, with specs on her face,
Big, beautiful eyes filled with grace.
~summry

My Girl in Glasses

She’s sitting there with glasses on her face,
Big eyes bright, filled with grace.

In every glance, I see the world,
And in her smile, my heart’s unfurled.

~summry

No Name

In the quiet, I search for you,
In every shadow, in every view.
But you’re not here, not in the crowd,
Not in the whispers that are too loud.

Yet somehow, I know you’re there,
Hidden behind the silent air.

~summry
